LESSON ZERO

Before You Place a Single Trade

The rules that separate people who survive the market from people who don't.

What Assets to Trade

✅ High-Volume Single Stocks

NVDA, TSLA, AAPL — highly liquid names with tight spreads and strong intraday momentum. One ticker at a time, fully focused. The strategy is depth over breadth: know one name's behaviour better than anyone.

✅ Major Index ETFs

SPY, QQQ. Diversified by nature — one bad earnings report from a single company cannot collapse them. Good for learning market structure before focusing on single names.

⚠️ Know the Risk First

Single stocks can gap 10–15% overnight on earnings, analyst downgrades, or geopolitical headlines. On a $100 account that is significant. Always set your stop loss before the session ends. Never hold over the weekend without a plan.

How Much to Risk Per Trade

The 1% Rule — your most important number

Never risk more than 1% of your total account on a single trade. On a $100 account, that is $1.00 maximum loss per trade. This feels tiny. That is the point. It keeps you alive long enough to learn.

Trades before total wipeout

Position size = Max risk ÷ Distance to stop loss. If NVDA is at $220 and your stop is $217.80 (1% below), your max risk is $2.20/share. With $1 max loss you can trade 0.45 shares. On eToro fractional shares make this possible from day one. Use leverage carefully — it multiplies losses just as fast as gains.

The Rules — Do and Don't

✅ Do

Set your stop loss before entering

Decide your exit point when you are calm — not when the position is moving against you and emotions take over.

Take profits at 1–3% and exit fully

Small consistent wins compound. The goal is not one big trade — it is 1,000 disciplined ones.

Trade the same day you enter

This strategy is intraday. Open and close the same session. Holding overnight introduces gap risk you cannot control.

Record every trade — win or lose

A trader without a log is guessing. A log shows you your actual edge, your worst habits, and your real win rate over time.

Use a demo account first

Practise execution, platform mechanics, and emotional responses before real money is involved.

❌ Don't

Hold a losing trade hoping it recovers

Hope is not a strategy. Every large loss started as a small loss that someone refused to take. The market does not know or care what price you paid.

Trade without a stop loss in place

This is the single most common way beginners blow up accounts. No stop = unlimited downside. Always.

Hold positions over the weekend

Markets are closed but the world keeps moving. Geopolitical events, central bank announcements, and company news can cause large gaps at Monday's open that your stop cannot protect against.

Risk more after a losing trade

Revenge trading — doubling down to recover losses quickly — is the fastest path to losing everything. Losing streaks are normal. Increasing size during them is fatal.

Confuse a lucky win with skill

One profitable trade proves nothing. Even a random strategy wins sometimes. Judge yourself over at least 50 trades, not 5.
