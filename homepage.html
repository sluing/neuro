AXLE — Principia Orthogona

The generative backbone of the system.

Orientation

What AXLE Is

AXLE is a minimal, generative environment built to explore the structural scales of Principia Orthogona. It is not a theorem engine, not a proof program, and not a search for cosmic constants. AXLE is a behavioral system — a way to watch structures move, drift, stabilize, destabilize, and reorganize across scales.

The g‑Series

A Ladder of Scales — Not a Set of Constants

The g‑series is the backbone of AXLE. It is not a list of fixed numbers or universal laws. Each g‑level marks a behavioral scale the system may enter as its internal dynamics evolve.

  • g⁶ — the cycle-scale: free, oscillatory, unconstrained. A regime, not a return.
  • g³³ — a soft equilibrium region: a moving target with visible variance.
  • g⁶⁴ — the phase-change threshold: the boundary to a new descriptive layer.

These values are markers, not laws. They organize behavior without fixing it. In AXLE v6.1, all rigid invariants dissolve — there is no remaining theorem to prove. The system is free to move, drift, stabilize, destabilize, and reorganize across scales.
