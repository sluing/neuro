Why This Matters

Accident victims represent one of the most underserved populations in American medicine. Most physicians avoid them due to billing complexity — lien-based payment, delayed settlements, insurer disputes. NeuroSpine Plus operates in a high-impact, data-rich niche that the broader medical establishment has largely abandoned. That creates both a humanitarian opportunity and a research opportunity: a concentrated, well-documented cohort of neurological injury cases with no formal predictive infrastructure behind them.

The dm³ Framework

I am an independent researcher (and your patient) who has developed the dm³ contact-geometric framework — a formally verified mathematical model of biological threshold events. The operator chain G = U ∘ F ∘ K ∘ C models any system in which a continuous signal crosses a threshold and produces a discrete, irreversible output: a recovery milestone, a loss of function, or a clinical inflection point. The framework has ~970 machine-verified theorems across 53 Lean 4 files — all publicly available on GitHub (github.com/TOTOGT/AXLE), Zenodo, and live books — and has been validated across 5 biological domains including microtubule dynamics under anesthesia — a direct bridge to neurosurgical care.

The Neurology Connection

In TBI and spinal injury patients, dm³ predicts recovery follows a sigmoid curve with a mathematically derived inflection point:

  • Compression (C): acute injury compresses neurological function toward a critical threshold
  • Curvature threshold κ* = 0.882: the clinical tipping point — recovery accelerates above it, plateaus below it
  • Fold (F): discrete functional recovery — the moment a deficit resolves or becomes permanent
  • Unfolding (U): stabilization; preparation for the next recovery cycle

Falsifiable prediction: neurological recovery probability follows a sigmoid with Hill coefficient nH ≈ 3.64 (derived from geometry, not curve-fitting). Testable against publicly available NSCISC and TBIMS datasets — no proprietary patient data required for Phase I.
